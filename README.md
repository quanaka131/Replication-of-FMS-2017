# Replication-of-FMS-2017
A Replication of Geographic Dispersion of Economic Shocks: Evidence from the Fracking Revolution 

For my ECON 6901 (Research Methods for Economists) course, I replicated Feyrer, Mansur, and Sacerdote’s “Geographic Dispersion of Economic Shocks: Evidence from the Fracking Revolution” using Python instead of Stata, and matched the original results. I extended the analysis by adding richer summary statistics and clearer economic intuition to better motivate the 2SLS identification strategy. 

The results show that approximately 20% of the value of new production remains within the drilling county, while 54% accrues within a 100-mile radius. Roughly two-thirds of BLS wage gains persist for two years, driven largely by non-mining industries. Over a three-year horizon, counties retain about one-third of production value, whereas the surrounding 100-mile region captures more than 80% of the initial gains.
